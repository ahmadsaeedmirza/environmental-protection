ISSUES

CLIMATE CHANGE

Climate change refers to the long-term shift in global weather patterns and rising temperatures caused by greenhouse gas emissions from burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

Impacts:

  • Melting glaciers and rising sea levels threaten coastal cities.
  • More frequent and severe weather events like hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires.
  • Decline in crop yields, leading to food insecurity.

Statistics:

  • Global temperatures have risen by 1.2Â°C since pre-industrial levels.
  • The Arctic ice is shrinking by 13% per decade.
  • CO2 levels reached 417 ppm in 2023, the highest in 2 million years.

AIR POLLUTION

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances like carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter are released into the atmosphere, affecting air quality and public health.

Impacts:

  • Respiratory diseases like asthma and bronchitis.
  • Premature deaths due to air quality issues (7 million annually).
  • Damage to crops, reducing agricultural yields.

Statistics:

  • 99% of the global population breathes air that exceeds WHO air quality guidelines.
  • India and China have some of the world's most polluted cities (e.g., Delhi's AQI often exceeds 400).
  • Air pollution costs the global economy $8 trillion annually.

PLASTIC POLLUTION

Plastic pollution refers to the accumulation of plastic waste in the environment, causing harm to wildlife, ecosystems, and human health.

Impacts:

  • Kills marine life through ingestion and entanglement.
  • Breaks down into microplastics, which contaminate food and water.
  • Clogs waterways and creates breeding grounds for diseases.

Statistics:

  • Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans annually.
  • By 2050, there could be more plastic in the ocean than fish (by weight).
  • Only 9% of plastic waste ever produced has been recycled.

LOSS OF BIODIVERSITY

Biodiversity loss refers to the extinction of species due to habitat destruction, pollution, overhunting, and climate change.

Impacts:

  • Reduces ecosystem services like pollination, water purification, and climate regulation.
  • Weakens ecosystem resilience to environmental changes.
  • Affects food security and medicinal research.

Statistics:

  • 1 million species are at risk of extinction within decades.
  • Wildlife populations have declined by 69% since 1970.
  • 75% of global food crops depend on pollinators like bees, which are under threat.

DEFORESTATION

Deforestation is the clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, or urbanization, leading to habitat destruction and increased carbon emissions.

Impacts:

  • Loss of wildlife habitats and biodiversity.
  • Increased carbon dioxide levels, contributing to climate change.
  • Reduced rainfall and soil fertility.

Statistics:

  • The planet loses 10 million hectares of forest annually, equivalent to 27 soccer fields per minute.
  • Forests absorb 2.6 billion tons of CO2 annually, helping combat climate change.
  • 70% of Earthâs terrestrial animals and plants live in forests.

When We Can Save Earth

The Earth needs our help now more than ever. From the rise of pollution to the destruction of our forests, we face an environmental crisis. Your support can make a real difference in our efforts to restore and protect our planet for future generations.

OUR CONTRIBUTION

As part of our ongoing efforts to contribute to environmental protection, we undertook a variety of initiatives aimed at not only improving the immediate area but also raising awareness about the broader importance of sustainability. One of our primary actions was planting 20 trees in strategic locations. These trees serve multiple purposes: they absorb carbon dioxide, provide oxygen, enhance biodiversity by offering habitats for local wildlife, and contribute to the overall aesthetic and health of the community. By adding greenery to the environment, we hope to inspire others to take similar actions and create a more sustainable ecosystem.

In addition to planting trees, we designed and distributed awareness charts throughout the community. These charts were carefully crafted to educate people about the significant impact of their everyday choices on the environment. Topics covered included the importance of reducing waste, recycling materials, conserving water and energy, and protecting green spaces. The aim was not just to inform but to actively engage community members, encouraging them to take responsibility for their actions and adopt environmentally friendly practices in their daily lives.
